03 · How I Work
The Process.
Five stages
Four to twelve weeks
One operator throughout
01

Discovery & Brief

A conversation about what you're building, who it's for, and what the work needs to do. I read your existing assets, your competitors, your source texts. By the end of this stage, we have a shared understanding of what success looks like — measured in outcomes, not deliverables.

DurationWeek 1
02

Design Direction

Two or three concept directions, each shown as a full hero page rather than a moodboard. You see the type system, the colour, the motion language, and the editorial register all at once. We pick one direction and refine, rather than mixing and diluting.

DurationWeek 2 — 3
03

Build & Iteration

The site or application is built in production from week one — no Figma-to-handoff gap. You see real progress on a staging URL. We iterate in code, not in mockups. Performance, accessibility, and semantic structure are part of the build, not retrofits.

DurationWeek 3 — 8
04

Launch & Handover

We ship to production together, with a checklist that covers SEO, analytics, performance budgets, and content workflow. You receive a documented codebase and a CMS your team can use without needing me on call for every change.

DurationWeek 8 — 10
05

Ongoing Refinement

Optional. A monthly retainer for brands that want continuous improvement — A/B tests, new sections, animation polish, performance work, design system evolution. Most clients move into this after launch because the work compounds.

DurationMonthly · Optional
·

A note on timing

The brief above assumes a substantial scope — a complete brand and site for a serious house. Smaller engagements move faster. A single landing page might ship in two weeks, an application in six. The constraint is rarely calendar time; it's how many decisions can move through one head per week without the work losing coherence.

ConstraintOne operator
